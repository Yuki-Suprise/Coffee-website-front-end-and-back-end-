Origin of coffee

The skin of coffee fruit is very thick and tastes slightly bitter.
The core under the peel is sweet, and its texture is similar to that of grapes.
The inner layer of the kernel is a protective film for the seed.
This film is much like the protective film of apple seeds.
Remove this film and you'll see green coffee beans - usually two. After washing the coffee beans, you can bake them.

Coffee baking

After baking in a container for 10 to 11 minutes, the coffee beans become darker and darker, and oil begins to emerge on the surface. At this baking stage (usually 11 or 15 minutes depending on the type of coffee), the strong flavor of the coffee beans begins to be fully released and perfectly integrated with the special taste of the coffee beans themselves.
